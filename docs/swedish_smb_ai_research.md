# Quick Research: Swedish SMB AI Adoption & Gaps (2026)

## Current State: Adoption Rates

**Overall Enterprise AI Adoption:**
- **35% of Swedish enterprises** (≥10 employees) now use AI in 2025, up from 10.4% in 2023 — **236% increase** in 2 years
- Sweden ranks **3rd in EU** for AI adoption (behind Denmark 42%, Finland 38%)
- **Large enterprises lead**: 71.9% adoption
- **SMEs catching up** but significant digital divide remains

**SMB-Specific Data:**
- **47% of SMBs are using AI** (Bredin 2026 survey of 500 SMB principals)
- **58% plan to increase AI spending in 2026**
- AI usage increases sharply with headcount: **73% of mid-sized businesses** (100+ employees) use AI vs smaller SMBs

**By Sector:**
- ICT companies: **87.9% adoption** (highest)
- Business services: 90%+ optimistic outlook
- Construction & energy: More cautious

---

## Most Common Use Cases

**Currently Delivering Results:**
- Process automation and administrative task streamlining
- **74% of Swedish companies have achieved lower costs through AI** primarily via:
  - Increased process automation
  - Streamlining administrative tasks
  - Efficiency improvements (faster processes, reduced costs)

**Popular Applications:**
- Customer service automation
- IT operations and employee services
- Data analysis and forecasting
- Content generation

**Key Pattern:**
AI is primarily seen as a **streamlining tool** rather than innovation driver — companies focus on measurable efficiency gains.

---

## Key Barriers & Challenges

### #1 Barrier: Lack of AI Expertise
- **74.7% of non-adopting companies** cite lack of AI expertise as the main barrier
- **88% of companies globally** report challenges finding skilled personnel
- Talent shortage is THE critical constraint

### Other Major Barriers:
- **Resource gaps** in smaller companies (<300 MSEK revenue)
- **Competence gaps** preventing scaling
- Lack of relevant use cases (11.1% of non-adopters)
- Integration complexity with legacy systems
- Governance concerns

### Size-Based Digital Divide:
- Larger companies (>1000 MSEK) see revenue effects more often
- Smaller companies (<300 MSEK) have highest share NOT using AI

---

## Identified Gaps

### 1. **Innovation vs Efficiency Trap**
Swedish companies treat AI as streamlining tool but miss innovation opportunities. Risk: AI used for cost-cutting rather than creating new value.

### 2. **Skills & Talent Gap**
Nearly 75% can't adopt AI due to lack of expertise. SMBs struggle to compete for AI talent. Need: accessible training, consultants, or pre-built solutions.

### 3. **SME-Specific Support**
Large enterprises (71.9% adoption) race ahead, SMEs lag. Need: SME-tailored AI solutions and support.

### 4. **Strategic vs Tactical Use**
Most adoption is tactical (automate admin) rather than strategic (transform business models).

### 5. **Use Case Identification**
11.1% of non-adopters say "lack of relevant use cases." They don't see HOW AI applies to their business.

### 6. **Scaling Beyond Pilots**
Companies pilot AI but struggle to scale to production due to integration, governance, and legacy systems.

## Swedish-Specific Context

**Positive Factors:**
- 70% of CEOs expect 2026 to be better economically
- 75% expect higher revenue in 2026
- Shift from cost-cutting to growth initiatives
- Swedish AI startups raised €454M in 2025 (3x previous year)
- SEK 40B invested in AI R&D in 2024

**Challenges:**
- Political uncertainty and geopolitical concerns
- Global trade tariffs impacting sectors
- Construction/energy sectors cautious

**Population Adoption:**
- 25% of Swedish population (2.1M people) have used GenAI
- 50% among ages 16-24
